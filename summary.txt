Cyrus Network – “Trust Without Borders”

Cyrus Network is a decentralized trust and settlement protocol designed to enable cross-border transactions without relying on banks or centralized intermediaries. It connects verified human brokers who can escrow and settle payments locally, creating a digital version of hawala built on transparency, accountability, and smart-contract logic.

By combining verifiable reputation, escrow automation, and peer-to-peer liquidity, Cyrus makes it possible for individuals and businesses in restricted or high-friction markets to exchange value securely — whether fiat, stablecoins, or digital credits — even where formal financial rails are unreliable or unavailable.

In essence: Cyrus Network replaces institutional trust with verifiable human trust, secured by technology.

Being non-custodial definitely helps, but unfortunately doesn't eliminate regulatory concerns. Here's why:

Still potential regulatory issues:

Facilitating money transmission: Even if you never touch funds, many jurisdictions regulate platforms that facilitate money transmission between parties. The brokers might be the ones moving money, but you're orchestrating it.
Money Service Business (MSB) definitions: In the US, for example, FinCEN has broad definitions that can include "any person doing business... in one or more of the following capacities" including money transmission facilitation.
Broker activities: Your brokers are clearly money transmitters in most jurisdictions. By organizing them into a network, you could be seen as operating an unlicensed money transmission network.
Recent precedents:
LocalBitcoins (non-custodial P2P) still had to implement full KYC
Tornado Cash developers were sanctioned despite being "just code"
Various P2P platforms have faced enforcement actions
Potential regulatory advantages of your approach:

Easier to argue you're just a software/communication layer
Decentralization could make enforcement harder
Smart contract automation reduces your active role
What might actually help:

Operating purely as a reputation/communication protocol (like a "Yelp for brokers")
Brokers being licensed in their jurisdictions
Clear separation between protocol and any company operations
Starting in crypto-friendly jurisdictions
I'd bump my rating to 7.5/10 if truly non-custodial, but regulatory risk remains substantial. The question becomes: are regulators willing to distinguish between the protocol layer and money transmission activity? Recent history suggests they often aren't.

what is hawala?


Synthesized comprehensive explanation of hawala mechanics and relevance.

Hawala is an ancient informal money transfer system that moves billions annually without banks or wire transfers.

How it works:

Simple example:

Ali in Dubai wants to send $1000 to his family in Mumbai
Ali gives $1000 cash to Hassan (Dubai hawala broker)
Hassan calls/texts Raj (Mumbai hawala broker): "Give $1000 to Ali's family"
Raj gives cash to Ali's family immediately
Hassan now owes Raj $1000
The magic: No money actually crossed borders. It's just IOUs between brokers.

Settlement happens later:

Maybe Raj has a customer sending money Dubai→Mumbai
Maybe they settle through trade (Raj exports textiles to Hassan)
Maybe they meet at a wedding and settle debts
Sometimes they just track running balances for months
Why it exists:

Predates banking by 1000+ years (started on Silk Road)
Faster than banks (minutes vs days)
Cheaper (2-3% vs 7-10% bank fees)
Works where banks don't (rural areas, conflict zones)
No paperwork needed
Trust is everything:

Brokers build reputation over generations
Breaking trust means exile from network
Often ethnic/family networks (Pakistani, Somali, etc.)
"Honor system" backed by community enforcement
Legal status:

Legal in UAE, India (with regulations)
Grey area in many countries
Illegal when used to evade taxes/sanctions
Post-9/11 scrutiny increased dramatically
Why Cyrus is "digital hawala": You're replacing phone calls with smart contracts, and family trust with cryptographic verification, but the core insight remains: moving value without moving money.
